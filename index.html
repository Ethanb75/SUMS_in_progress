Why Choose IEN?


Any department, shared user facility or IRI on campus can enter their equipment and immediately it is searchable by researchers.


Payments utilizing PeopleSoft project numbers can be automatically sent to the GT Controllers office for processing through SUMS. For larger equipment groups this can free hours of staff time per billing period.


Within SUMS trainers can configure the requirements for access to equipment including online tests, video's, training sessions and checkoffs. All researcher and trainer notification and record keeping is done within the SUMS framework.


The SUMS framework automates many of the tasks needed for a shared user facility operation to help streamline operations at no cost.


Physically interlock tools so that only trained and approved researchers can use them and capture this time directly for billing and reporting purposes.


The SUMS system directly interfaces with the GT ORS radiation/laser safety database for instant granting or removal of permission. Along with physically interlocking tools this assures that only trained and authorized researchers can use equipment.


The SUMS system provides equipment and billing reporting abilities to allow equipment group administrators the ability to quickly view who is using their equipment.


The SUMS framework offers a solution for the full life cycle of tool usage from researchers searching for equipment or services, training, billing and processing of payments for this usage all in one framework common across the GT campus.


The SUMS online scheduler allows equipment group personnell to configure scheduling rules for a variety of parameters for researchers scheduling time on their equipment including the ability for schedule requests to require staff approval. Queue is also available for walk-up type equipment.
